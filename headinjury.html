Follow these steps to properly treat a minor head injury:

  1. Stay calm and assess the situation.
  2. Check for any visible wounds. If there is bleeding, apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth to stop it.
  3. If the person is conscious, have them rest and sit upright to prevent any dizziness.
  4. Apply a cold compress or ice pack to the injured area to reduce swelling and pain. Wrap the ice in a cloth to avoid direct contact with the skin.
  5. Monitor for any signs of dizziness, confusion, or nausea. If these symptoms occur, seek medical attention immediately.
  6. If the injury was caused by a fall or impact, and the person loses consciousness or exhibits signs of a concussion, seek emergency medical help right away.

If the injury appears more serious, or if you are unsure about the severity, don't hesitate to consult a healthcare professional.
